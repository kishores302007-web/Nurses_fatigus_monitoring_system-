# World Health Organization (WHO) Guidelines on Workforce Fatigue

## 1. Fatigue and Clinical Error Proximity
- Medical studies supported by the World Health Organization establish a direct correlation between fatigue and cognitive decline.
- Nurses working shifts longer than 12 hours face a **3x increase** in risk of making needle-stick, treatment, or drug dosage errors.
- Chronic sleep debt (accumulated rest deficit under 6 hours per day) degrades executive function similarly to blood alcohol concentrations of 0.05%.

## 2. Physiological Signal Markers of Fatigue
- **Heart Rate Variability (HRV)**: Decreasing SDNN (Standard Deviation of Normal-to-Normal intervals) indicates autonomic nervous system fatigue and high cardiovascular workload.
- **Galvanic Skin Response (GSR)**: High skin conductance (low resistance voltage) reflects sympathetic nervous activity, indicating acute stress and cognitive load.
- **Peripheral Skin Temperature**: Fluctuations in skin temperature can indicate circadian rhythm shifts and onset of sleepiness or dehydration.

## 3. Recommended Remediation Workflows
- **Power Naps**: Scheduled 20-minute naps during night shifts can recover focus and motor responses by 34%.
- **De-escalation**: Re-allocating high-acuity patients (e.g. ICU, trauma) to fresher staff when a nurse shows signs of burnout.

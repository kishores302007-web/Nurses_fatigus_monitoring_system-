# St. Jude Medical Center - Nurse Fatigue & Shift Limits Policy

**Document Ref:** SJ-HR-2025-09
**Effective Date:** January 15, 2025
**Scope:** All Nursing Staff, ICU, ER, Cardiology, and General Wards.

## 1. Core Shift Regulations
- **Maximum Shift Duration**: No nurse shall work a continuous shift exceeding twelve (12) consecutive hours, except under major disaster declarations.
- **Mandatory Rest Period**: A minimum of ten (10) consecutive hours of off-duty rest is strictly mandated between any two shifts.
- **Consecutive Workdays**: Nurses must not work more than four (4) consecutive shifts of 12 hours within any 7-day period.

## 2. Real-Time Fatigue Monitoring Thresholds
The hospital integrates bio-signals from wearable IoT sensors to compute a **Fatigue Score (0-100)**:
- **0 - 30 (Normal)**: Standard operation. No action required.
- **31 - 60 (Moderate)**: Fatigue warning. Nurse encouraged to take a 15-minute hydration and posture break.
- **61 - 80 (High)**: Alert triggered. Supervisor notified.
- **81 - 100 (Critical)**: Mandatory relief. The system triggers the **Smart Replacement Engine** to locate an alternative nurse.

## 3. Mandatory Swap and Replacement Criteria
When a nurse's Fatigue Score exceeds **75** continuously for more than 15 minutes, or spikes past **90**, they must be relieved immediately:
- The Smart Replacement Engine selects candidates based on:
  1. Presence: Must be checked-in on-site.
  2. Qualification: Match the identical skill category (e.g. Critical Care for ICU).
  3. Physical Readiness: Candidate's own Fatigue Score must be below **40**.
  4. Work History: Total hours worked in past 24 hours must be under **10 hours**.
- The swap takes place automatically in the scheduling ledger upon supervisor approval.
